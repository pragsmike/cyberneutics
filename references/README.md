# References

This is the master bibliography for the Cyberneutics methodology. Every source cited or recommended across the essays, palgebra documents, and artifacts is listed here with a brief annotation and a pointer to where it appears in the repository.

Sources are organized by theoretical tradition. For a reading-path view (which sources to prioritize for which audience), see the [Further Reading section](../essays/README.md#for-further-reading) of the essays directory.

---

## Narrative & Cognition

**Bruner, Jerome S.** *Actual Minds, Possible Worlds* (1986). Harvard University Press. — Establishes the paradigmatic/narrative distinction: two irreducible cognitive modes that neither reduces to the other. The foundational claim that narrative is a primary cognitive mode, not a decorative one. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md), [Boland's Narrative Engineering](../essays/07-bolands-narrative-engineering.md), [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).

**Kahneman, Daniel.** *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011). Farrar, Straus and Giroux. — System 1 (fast, associative) and System 2 (slow, deliberate) as the neural implementation of Bruner's two modes. Grounds the LLM-as-System-1 framing. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).

**Beach, Lee Roy.** *The Psychology of Narrative Thought: How the Stories We Tell Ourselves Shape Our Lives* (2010). Xlibris. — Psychological treatment of narrative as the dominant medium of human cognition, with implications for decision-making. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).

**Pennington, Nancy, and Reid Hastie.** "Explaining the Evidence: Tests of the Story Model for Juror Decision Making." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* 62(2): 189–206 (1992). — Empirical evidence that humans organize decision-relevant information into narrative structures even in formal high-stakes contexts. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).

**Piper, Andrew, Richard Jean So, and David Bamman.** "Narrative Theory for Computational Narrative Understanding." *Proceedings of EMNLP 2021*, 298–311. — Bridges literary narrative theory and computational methods; grounds the computational narrative intelligence lineage. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).

**Riedl, Mark O.** "Computational Narrative Intelligence: A Human-Centered Goal for Artificial Intelligence." arXiv:1602.06484 (2016). — Argues for narrative understanding as the appropriate target for human-centered AI. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).

## Sense-Making

**Dervin, Brenda.** "From the Mind's Eye of the User: The Sense-Making Qualitative-Quantitative Methodology." In *Qualitative Research in Information Management*, eds. J. Glazier & R. Powell (1992). — The primary methodological statement of Sense-Making: gap, situation, bridge. Cited in: [Sensemaking 101](../essays/03-sensemaking-101.md), [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

**Dervin, Brenda.** "Sense-Making Theory and Practice: An Overview of User Interests in Knowledge Seeking and Use." *Journal of Knowledge Management* 2(2): 36–46 (1998). — Overview of the Sense-Making framework with emphasis on the active, constructive nature of information-seeking. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md), [Sensemaking 101](../essays/03-sensemaking-101.md).

**Foreman-Wernet, Lois.** "Rethinking Communication: Introducing the Sense-Making Methodology." In *Sense-Making Methodology Reader*, eds. B. Dervin & L. Foreman-Wernet (2003). — Accessible introduction and overview of Dervin's sense-making methodology and its research applications. Cited in: [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

## Cybernetics

**von Foerster, Heinz.** *Understanding Understanding: Essays on Cybernetics and Cognition* (2003). Springer. — Key essays on second-order cybernetics and the observer problem. The epigraph "Objectivity is the delusion that observations could be made without an observer" is from this collection. Cited in: [Cybernetics and the Observer Problem](../essays/04-cybernetics-and-observation.md), [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md), [Boland's Narrative Engineering](../essays/07-bolands-narrative-engineering.md).

**von Foerster, Heinz.** "Cybernetics of Cybernetics." In *Communication and Control in Society*, ed. K. Krippendorff (1979). — The foundational statement of second-order cybernetics: the shift from observing systems to systems that observe themselves. Cited in: [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

**Bateson, Gregory.** *Steps to an Ecology of Mind* (1972). Chandler. — Collected essays developing the ecology of mind framework; foundational for understanding context, pattern, and the role of information in living systems. Cited in: [The Synthesis](../essays/05-the-synthesis.md), [Sensemaking 101](../essays/03-sensemaking-101.md), [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

**Pickering, Andrew.** *The Cybernetic Brain: Sketches of Another Future* (2010). University of Chicago Press. — Historical and philosophical study of the British cybernetics movement; connects cybernetics to performance, emergence, and anti-disciplinary practice. Cited in: [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

## Philosophy

**Deleuze, Gilles.** *Difference and Repetition* (1968; trans. Paul Patton, 1994). Columbia University Press. — The foundational philosophical text: reality as difference rather than identity, becoming rather than being. Grounds the Cyberneutics insight that repetition produces difference. Cited in: [Deleuzian Foundations](../essays/06-deleuze-difference-repetition.md), [Boland's Narrative Engineering](../essays/07-bolands-narrative-engineering.md).

**Deleuze, Gilles.** *Bergsonism* (1966; trans. Hugh Tomlinson & Barbara Habberjam, 1988). Zone Books. — Shorter and more accessible than *Difference and Repetition*; develops the becoming/duration framework central to understanding how LLM responses are events, not retrievals. Cited in: [Deleuzian Foundations](../essays/06-deleuze-difference-repetition.md).

**Deleuze, Gilles, and Félix Guattari.** *A Thousand Plateaus: Capitalism and Schizophrenia* (1987). University of Minnesota Press. — Plateau as a model for non-hierarchical, multiply-connected conceptual terrain. Cited in: [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

**DeLanda, Manuel.** *Intensive Science and Virtual Philosophy* (2002). Continuum. — The clearest exposition of Deleuze's relevance to science and formal thinking; makes the connection between Deleuzian philosophy and mathematical formalisms explicit. Cited in: [Deleuzian Foundations](../essays/06-deleuze-difference-repetition.md), [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

**Massumi, Brian.** *A User's Guide to Capitalism and Schizophrenia* (1992). MIT Press. — Readable introduction to Deleuze-Guattari; useful for understanding the conceptual vocabulary. Cited in: [Deleuzian Foundations](../essays/06-deleuze-difference-repetition.md).

**Uexküll, Jakob von.** *A Foray into the Worlds of Animals and Humans* (1934; trans. Joseph D. O'Neil, 2010). University of Minnesota Press. — The Umwelt concept: every organism inhabits a self-constructed perceptual world. Grounds the claim that LLMs don't share our world but construct their own response-worlds. Cited in: [Boland's Narrative Engineering](../essays/07-bolands-narrative-engineering.md).

**Kuhn, Thomas S.** *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions* (1962; 3rd ed. 1996). University of Chicago Press. — Paradigm shifts as the mechanism of scientific change; grounds the claim that LLMs represent a paradigm shift in how we think about cognition and computation. Cited in: [Boland's Narrative Engineering](../essays/07-bolands-narrative-engineering.md).

**Gödel, Kurt.** "Über formal unentscheidbare Sätze der Principia Mathematica und verwandter Systeme I." *Monatshefte für Mathematik und Physik* 38(1): 173–198 (1931). — Incompleteness theorems; grounds Boland's argument that formal systems cannot capture their own semantics, and hence that narrative (meaning) always exceeds syntax. Cited in: [Boland's Narrative Engineering](../essays/07-bolands-narrative-engineering.md).

## Systems & Life

**Rosen, Robert.** *Life Itself: A Comprehensive Inquiry into the Nature, Origin, and Fabrication of Life* (1991). Columbia University Press. — Demonstrates using category theory that living systems are not mechanism-simulable: complex systems require semantic description beyond syntactic manipulation. Grounds the theoretical necessity of narrative computing. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md), [references/README.md](./README.md).

## AI & LLMs

**Bender, Emily M., Timnit Gebru, Angelina McMillan-Major, and Shmargaret Shmitchell.** "On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots: Can Language Models Be Too Big?" *FAccT '21: Proceedings of the 2021 ACM Conference on Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency*, 610–623. — The "stochastic parrot" framing: LLMs produce plausible text without understanding; critical treatment of the risks of scale without grounding. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).

**Shanahan, Murray.** "Talking About Large Language Models." arXiv:2212.03551 (2022; revised 2024). — Careful philosophical analysis of what it means to attribute mental states to LLMs; argues for precise language about what these systems do and do not do. Cited in: [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).

**Kim, Janice, et al.** "Reasoning Models Generate Societies of Thought." arXiv:2601.10825 (2025). — Mechanistic interpretability study of DeepSeek-R1 and QwQ-32B showing that enhanced reasoning correlates with internal multi-agent dynamics (questioning, perspective shifts, conflict, reconciliation). Direct empirical validation of the adversarial committee methodology. Cited in: [Societies of Thought synthesis](../essays/societies-of-thought-synthesis.md).

## Metacognition & Judgment

**Fleming, Stephen M.** "HMeta-d: hierarchical Bayesian estimation of metacognitive efficiency from confidence ratings." *Neuroscience of Consciousness* 2017(1): nix007 (2017). https://doi.org/10.1093/nc/nix007. — Metacognition as the ability to reflect on and monitor one's cognitive processes. Introduces hierarchical Bayesian estimation of *metacognitive efficiency* (meta-d'/d'): how well an observer's confidence ratings predict objective success, controlling for type 1 performance (d') and response bias. meta-d' is the sensitivity of confidence to correct/incorrect; meta-d'/d' &lt; 1 indicates suboptimal use of available information when reporting confidence. HMeta-d outperforms point-estimate methods when data are limited. **Software (canonical):** [metacoglab/HMeta-d](https://github.com/metacoglab/HMeta-d) (MATLAB and R; JAGS). **Tutorial:** [HMeta-d tutorial (wiki)](https://github.com/metacoglab/HMeta-d/wiki/HMeta-d-tutorial). Cited in: [Metacognition and committee veracity](../artifacts/metacognition-and-committee-veracity.md).

**Maniscalco, Brian, and Hakwan Lau.** "A signal detection theoretic approach for estimating metacognitive sensitivity from confidence ratings." *Consciousness and Cognition* 21(1): 422–430 (2012). — Original meta-d' model: confidence reports modelled within signal detection theory; meta-d' recovered by inverting the type 2 ROC. Grounds bias-free measurement of metacognitive sensitivity. Cited in: [Metacognition and committee veracity](../artifacts/metacognition-and-committee-veracity.md).

**Fleming, Stephen M., and Hakwan C. Lau.** "How to measure metacognition." *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience* 8: 443 (2014). — Review of confidence–accuracy approaches and why simple correlations are confounded; meta-d' as the principled alternative. Cited in: [Metacognition and committee veracity](../artifacts/metacognition-and-committee-veracity.md).

## Category Theory & Formalism

**Fong, Brendan, and David I. Spivak.** *Seven Sketches in Compositionality: An Invitation to Applied Category Theory* (2019). Cambridge University Press. — Resource theories (Chapter 2) and string diagrams for symmetric monoidal categories provide the mathematical foundation for the palgebra formalism. Cited in: [palgebra/reference.md](../palgebra/reference.md), [palgebra/decorated-texts.md](../palgebra/decorated-texts.md).

**Fong, Brendan.** *The Algebra of Open and Interconnected Systems* (2016). PhD thesis, University of Oxford. — Decorated cospans for composing open systems; grounds the treatment of LLM pipelines as composable open systems. Cited in: [palgebra/reference.md](../palgebra/reference.md).

**Kelly, G. Maxwell.** *Basic Concepts of Enriched Category Theory* (1982). Cambridge University Press. — The technical foundation for enrichment over confidence lattices in the palgebra. Cited in: [palgebra/reference.md](../palgebra/reference.md).

**Baez, John, and Mike Stay.** "Physics, Topology, Logic and Computation: A Rosetta Stone." In *New Structures for Physics*, ed. B. Coecke (2011). Springer. — String diagrams as a unified language across physics, logic, and computation. Cited in: [palgebra/README.md](../palgebra/README.md).

**Lawvere, F. William, and Stephen Schanuel.** *Conceptual Mathematics: A First Introduction to Categories* (1997). Cambridge University Press. — Accessible introduction to category theory emphasizing conceptual over technical; recommended entry point for the palgebra formalism. Cited in: [Deleuzian Foundations](../essays/06-deleuze-difference-repetition.md).

**Spivak, David I.** *Category Theory for the Sciences* (2014). MIT Press. — Applications-focused introduction to category theory; bridges formal machinery and scientific domains. Cited in: [Deleuzian Foundations](../essays/06-deleuze-difference-repetition.md).

## Applied Human-Computer Interaction

**Winograd, Terry, and Fernando Flores.** *Understanding Computers and Cognition: A New Foundation for Design* (1986). Ablex. — Applies Heideggerian and second-order cybernetic insights to computer system design; argues that understanding cannot be separated from the context of use. Cited in: [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

**Suchman, Lucy.** *Human-Machine Reconfigurations: Plans and Situated Actions* (2nd ed., 2007). Cambridge University Press. — Ethnomethodological critique of AI planning systems; demonstrates that human action is situationally produced rather than plan-executed. Cited in: [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md).

**Clark, Andy.** *Supersizing the Mind: Embodiment, Action, and Cognitive Extension* (2008). Oxford University Press. — The extended mind thesis: cognitive processes extend beyond brain and body into tools and environments. Grounds the treatment of LLMs as cognitive extensions rather than oracles. Cited in: [essays/README.md](../essays/README.md) (and planned essay: *Narrative Computing and the Extended Mind*).

## Agentic AI & Software Methodology

**De Wynter, Adrian, et al.** "On Meta-Prompting." arXiv:2312.06562 (2023). — Category-theoretic framework for LLM interactions; independent convergence on compositional prompt structure. Cited in: [palgebra/reference.md](../palgebra/reference.md), [palgebra/README.md](../palgebra/README.md).

**Liang, Guanlin, et al.** "Prompts Are Programs Too!" arXiv:2409.12447 (2024; accepted FSE 2025). — Empirical study of prompt development as a programming phenomenon; developers use structured workflows analogous to software engineering. Cited in: [palgebra/reference.md](../palgebra/reference.md), [palgebra/README.md](../palgebra/README.md).

**Hopkins, Don.** MOOLLM. https://github.com/SimHacker/MOOLLM. — Governance framework for LLMs providing structured environments ("worlds," "rooms," "cards," "files") where AI interactions are constrained by explicit rules, roles, and protocols. Referenced in: [From Practice to Theory](../essays/02-from-practice-to-theory.md), [research-plan.md](../meta/research-plan.md).

**StrongDM.** "Software Factories and the Agentic Moment." https://factory.strongdm.ai/. — Industry perspective on agentic AI systems as software factories; useful framing for the infrastructure implications of narrative computing at scale. Referenced in: [references/README.md](./README.md).

**StrongDM.** Attractor. https://github.com/strongdm/attractor. — NL-spec-driven software factory implementation; reference implementation for agentic coding patterns. Referenced in: [references/README.md](./README.md).

**Rasheed, Haris, et al.** "Agentsway: Software Development Methodology for AI Agents-based Teams." arXiv:2510.23664 (2025). — Proposes a structured methodology for AI-agent-based software development teams; relevant to the formalization of Cyberneutics workflows. Referenced in: [references/README.md](./README.md).
