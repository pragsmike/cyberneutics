# References

This directory contains background reading and theoretical foundations for the Cyberneutics methodology.

For a full annotated bibliography organized by theoretical tradition (Sense-Making, Cybernetics, Deleuze, Applied Works), see the [Further Reading section](../essays/README.md#for-further-reading) of the essays directory. Additional citations appear in [Narrative Computing as Historical Progression](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md#references) and [Deleuzian Foundations](../essays/06-deleuze-difference-repetition.md#further-reading).

## Key Sources

### Theory

*   **Dervin's Sense-Making Methodology**: Resources on Brenda Dervin's situation-gap-bridge model. Introduced in [Essay 03](../essays/03-sensemaking-101.md).
*   **Second-Order Cybernetics**: Works by Heinz von Foerster, Gregory Bateson, and others on the role of the observer in systems. Introduced in [Essay 04](../essays/04-cybernetics-and-observation.md).
*   **Deleuzian Philosophy**: Concepts of becoming, difference, and repetition relevant to narrative computing. Introduced in [Essay 06](../essays/06-deleuze-difference-repetition.md).
*   **[Narrative Engineering](https://alexbo.land/essays/narrative_engineering_2023.html)** (Alex Boland): Foundational essay on steering narrative potential using diegetic frames. Discussed in [Essay 07](../essays/07-bolands-narrative-engineering.md).
*   **Life Itself** (Robert Rosen): Comprehensive proof that mechanism (numeric/symbolic) cannot simulate living systems (narrative/semantic), necessitating a different category of modeling for complex systems. Discussed in [Narrative Computing History](../essays/narrative-computing-history.md).
*   **Seven Sketches in Compositionality** (Fong & Spivak): Applied category theory and resource theories that ground the [Palgebra formalism](../palgebra/decorated-texts.md).
